## 7. ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS FOR TRADERS

1. **The recovery story is real but fragile:** Revenue and margins are improving sequentially, and net income has been positive for four straight quarters after the Q4 2024 loss. However, the quality of earnings is somewhat mixed (unusual items helped Q4 2025).

2. **Debt-fueled growth is a double-edged sword:** The massive debt increase may fund productive capex that drives future earnings, but it also raises the break-even coal price for the company. If thermal coal prices decline, interest coverage could become strained (current interest expense is IDR 12.8B/quarter, roughly 48% of Q1 2026 operating income).

3. **Valuation is not supportive at current levels:** A P/E of 43x is pricing in perfection. For context, if coal prices mean-revert even modestly, earnings could contract sharply — the company lost IDR 53.1B in Q4 2024 when revenues were actually higher (IDR 432.8B) than current levels, driven by cost pressures.

4. **Watch for the Q3 2025 gap:** There is a notable data gap for Q3 2025 in the quarterly filings. The absence of this data may be innocuous or could signal reporting issues — worth investigating.

5. **Key levels for traders:** The stock at IDR 185 sits between its 52-week low (103) and its moving averages (~222–224). A break above the 200-day MA (~224) could signal a trend reversal. Conversely, a break below 150 could open the path to retest the 103 low.

**Bottom Line:** PT Bumi Resources Tbk presents a classic commodity turnaround narrative — improving operations, recovering margins, and returning to profitability — but this is overshadowed by an alarming debt accumulation, persistently negative free cash flow, and a stretched valuation. The stock may appeal to aggressive traders betting on sustained coal price strength, but the fundamental risk-reward profile, given leverage levels and the 43x P/E multiple, appears unfavorable at current prices. A conservative stance would be to wait for either a meaningful pullback in the stock price, a demonstrable reduction in leverage, or a return to positive free cash flow before considering an entry.

---

**FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: SELL**

The combination of critically high and rapidly rising leverage (Debt/Equity 19.97), three consecutive quarters of negative free cash flow, a stretched P/E of 43.3x relative to coal peers, and the stock trading below both its 50-day and 200-day moving averages supports a bearish fundamental outlook. The debt-funded cash position is unsustainable, and any softening in coal prices would expose significant downside risk to both earnings and the stock price.
