# BUMI.JK (PT Bumi Resources Tbk) — Comprehensive Research Report
**Date: May 23, 2026 | Period Under Review: May 16–23, 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

BUMI.JK finds itself at the eye of a perfect storm. On one side, a **war-driven coal demand surge** and strong company fundamentals provide powerful tailwinds. On the other, a **radical government export centralization policy** and a **currency crisis** have created a panic-driven sell-off in Indonesian equities. The net effect is extreme uncertainty, making BUMI a high-risk/high-reward proposition for traders right now.

---

## 1. Company-Specific Developments (BUMI Resources)

### Strong Operational Performance

Bumi Resources has demonstrated impressive operational resilience:

- **Q1 2026**: Posted **strong profit growth** despite lower coal prices (Petromindo, May 5, 2026). This follows **FY 2025 net profit jumping 20.1%** despite the weak coal price environment (idnfinancials.com, March 30, 2026).
- **2026 Sales Target**: BUMI is targeting coal sales of **78 million tonnes** in 2026 (idnfinancials.com, December 2025).
- **Australian Expansion**: BUMI began production in Australia with **Glencore set to be the buyer** (idnfinancials.com, April 7, 2026). This diversifies BUMI's geographic exposure and locks in a blue-chip offtake partner.
- **Output Quota Protection**: In February 2026, the Indonesian government **spared major miners including BUMI from significant production cuts**, with the burden shifted to smaller firms (Bloomberg, February 4, 2026).
- **Financing**: BUMI is securing a **US$600 million loan** for expansion and underground mining projects, and plans further bond sales to refinance debt.

### Key Takeaway
BUMI's underlying business is in solid shape — growing production, strong profit conversion, and geographic diversification. The company is executing well operationally.

## 2. Global Macro Tailwinds: Coal Demand Surge

### The Iran War = Coal Renaissance

The ongoing US-Israel war with Iran has disrupted LNG supplies through the Strait of Hormuz, creating a massive energy shock across Asia:

- **Japan and South Korea** have ramped up coal-fired power generation in April and into early May as LNG supply disruptions intensify (Reuters, May 12, 2026).
- One column noted that thermal coal prices surged as much as **78% after Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion** — the Iran conflict may produce similar dynamics (Mining.com).
- Oil prices remain **above US$100/barrel**, making coal more economically attractive for power generation.
- The theme of **"permanent demand destruction for oil, case for coal"** is gaining traction (Globe and Mail/Motley Fool, May 2026).

### New Hope Corporation (Australian Coal Miner)
- Reported **strong Q3 FY2026 results**, with coal rally boosting EBITDA significantly (discoveryalert.com.au, ~May 19, 2026).
- This confirms that the thermal coal price rally is real and flowing through to producer earnings.

### Key Takeaway
As the world's largest thermal coal exporter, Indonesia — and by extension BUMI — should be a prime beneficiary of the coal demand surge. The macro backdrop for coal is the most bullish it has been since 2022.

## 3. Indonesia-Specific Headwinds: The Triple Shock

### Shock #1: Export Centralization — The "Danantara" Policy

**This is the single biggest risk facing BUMI.JK right now.**

On **May 20, 2026**, President Prabowo Subianto unveiled a sweeping plan to **centralize all coal, palm oil, and ferroalloy exports** through a new state-controlled entity — PT Danantara Sumber Daya Indonesia (PT DSI), supervised by the sovereign wealth fund Danantara. The scheme is set to begin **June 1, 2026**.

Key details:
- All coal exports must flow through the state agency.
- Danantara says existing contracts will be **honored**, but will be reviewed to ensure prices are "not below market levels" (Reuters, May 21).
- The policy was approved **just one day before its mandate was announced**, raising severe readiness concerns (Business Times, May 22).
- Nickel pig iron and some palm oil derivatives have been exempted (Reuters, May 22).

**Market Reaction:**
- "Indonesia coal export shake-up **rattles miners, traders**" (Reuters, May 22)
- "Indonesia's radical export experiment **upends its commodity trade**" (Bloomberg, May 20)
- The Jakarta Composite Index (IHSG/JCI) **sank 3.5%** on export policy fears (Jakarta Globe, ~May 19)
- Described as a "hostile takeover of major industries" (Washington Post, May 22)

**Implications for BUMI:**
- Margin compression risk if the state agency takes a cut
- Uncertainty about contract renegotiation timing
- Potential disruption to export logistics from June 1
- However, BUMI's Australian production (with Glencore as buyer) may be partially insulated

### Shock #2: Rupiah Crisis

The rupiah has collapsed to historic lows:
- Reached **Rp17,600–17,706 per USD** (Tempo.co, May 16–20, 2026)
- Driven by: Iran war oil spike, Fed leadership change, global risk-off sentiment
- Forbes described it as: *"Currencies Gone Wild? Welcome To Asia's Year Of Living Dangerously"* (May 22, 2026)

### Shock #3: Bank Indonesia's Emergency Rate Hike

- On **May 20**, BI surprised markets with a **50 bps rate hike** — the first hike in over two years and larger than the 25 bps expected.
- This is meant to defend the rupiah, but will **increase borrowing costs** for companies with USD-denominated debt (relevant for BUMI's US$600M loan plans).

### Shock #4: MSCI Cull & Capital Flight

- **MSCI removed six Indonesian companies** from its index on May 13, triggering outflows (Reuters)
- Broader theme of **"Capital Flight vs. Deep Value"** in Jakarta markets (InvestorTrust, May 19)

## Conclusion

BUMI.JK presents a **classic risk-on/risk-off dilemma**. The operational fundamentals and global coal demand backdrop are the strongest in years, yet the domestic policy shock (export centralization) and currency crisis introduce existential uncertainty for Indonesian coal exporters. The June 1 deadline for the Danantara export scheme is the key near-term catalyst — clarity (or lack thereof) will likely determine the stock's direction. The market is pricing in significant distress, which could represent deep value if the export policy is implemented pragmatically, or further downside if execution is heavy-handed.
