### 4. Catalysts and Risks Surfaced by the Data

**Catalysts (potential upside)**
- Nvidia’s record Q1 and bullish AI outlook continue to pull TSM higher (Asian chip rally).
- AMD’s 2nm EPYC “Venice” ramp volume production — directly benefits TSMC.
- Lam Research adding AI to chipmaking tools strengthens TSMC’s ecosystem.
- Geopolitical stability (if Trump/Taiwan deal is seen as reducing tension) could remove a valuation discount.

**Risks (potential downside)**
- **Rotation risk**: News explicitly states investors are looking beyond TSMC to names like MediaTek and Samsung. If this narrative accelerates, TSM could lag even as the AI theme broadens.
- **Geopolitical escalation**: Nikki Haley’s warning, Trump’s weapons pause — any fresh Taiwan Strait tension could hit TSM disproportionately.
- **Intel foundry narrative**: Intel’s 3x YTD rally is partly built on perceptions it could take share from TSMC. While still speculative, it’s a persistent headwind to sentiment.
- **Growth slowdown perception**: The IDVO ETF article and other cautious notes assume TSMC’s growth will eventually decelerate — if AI capex peaks, TSM is exposed.
