**Rationale**: The bull analyst carried this debate on the weight of evidence. The forward P/E of 20.7x—against 40%+ earnings growth, 46.5% net margins, 36.2% ROE, and a 90%+ monopoly on advanced nodes—makes a compelling case that TSMC is undervalued even near all-time highs. The bear's concerns about growth sustainability are well-argued, but the bull effectively demonstrated that even at a decelerated 30% growth rate, the forward multiple would expand only to ~24x, which is still cheap for a franchise of this quality. The rotation narrative was convincingly reframed: capital flowing into MediaTek, AMD, and other AI names actually increases demand for TSMC's capacity—the entire ecosystem routes through their fabs. On technicals, the MACD histogram is improving and RSI is neutral; this looks like healthy consolidation within a powerful uptrend, not a topping pattern. The bear's two genuinely weighty points are geopolitical tail risk and the possibility of margin compression from cyclical peaks. The geopolitical discount is real—TSMC trades at 20.7x forward when an equivalent US-based monopoly would command 30x+—and that discount provides the margin of safety. Margins may moderate but the product mix shift toward premium 3nm/2nm and CoWoS packaging argues for structurally higher profitability than pre-AI eras. The bull's case rests on data; the bear's rests on uncertainty. The data wins. We rate TSM Overweight rather than a full Buy because the geopolitical tail risk, while priced in at current levels, remains asymmetric and warrants measured position sizing. Recommend gradually building exposure, using any pullbacks toward the 50-day SMA (~$373) as opportunistic add points.
