## 3. Critical Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Developments

### A. 🇮🇩 INDONESIA'S COMMODITY EXPORT CENTRALIZATION (THE BIGGEST RISK)

**What happened (May 20, 2026):** President Prabowo Subianto announced that exports of **palm oil, coal, and ferroalloys** must be conducted through a single state agency (Danantara). This represents a sweeping overhaul of Indonesia's trade policies—described as a "hostile takeover" of major industries.

**For BUMI specifically:**
- Danantara will **honor existing export contracts** but will **review prices** to ensure they are not below market levels
- This creates uncertainty about future pricing freedom and margin compression
- Businesses and investors are "rattled"—described as "Prabowo's biggest crackdown on tycoons" (Straits Times, May 21)
- Coal sector stocks broadly fell on the news

**Exemptions (May 22):** Nickel pig iron and some palm oil derivatives were exempted, but **coal was NOT exempted**, keeping pressure on BUMI.

### B. IRAN WAR — THERMAL COAL PRICE TAILWIND

The ongoing Iran war (US/Israel vs Iran) has disrupted LNG supplies from the Middle East, causing Asian buyers (Japan, South Korea) to **switch from gas to coal** for power generation:

- Seaborne thermal coal prices have rallied in Asia
- Newcastle coal prices surged, though gains are more modest than the 2022 Russia-Ukraine crisis
- "How the Iran War Is Pushing Thermal Coal Prices Higher in 2026" (Discovery Alert, May 6)
- Reuters (May 5): "Iran war gives small boost to thermal coal, further gains possible"
- New Hope Corp (ASX) reported strong Q3 FY2026 results with coal rally boosting EBITDA (May 19)
- Glencore positioned to benefit from Newcastle coal price surge

**This is a net positive for BUMI as higher coal prices improve margins.**

### C. INDONESIA COAL PRODUCTION QUOTA — BUMI SPARED

In February 2026, Bloomberg reported that **Indonesia's largest coal miners were spared from major output cuts**, with the production quota burden falling on smaller firms. BUMI was explicitly named among those spared. This allowed BUMI to target higher 2026 sales of 76–78 million tons.

### D. ENERGY POLICY — COAL USE MAINTAINED

Indonesia's Energy Minister confirmed (May 2, 2026) that the country will continue relying on coal-fired power plants to ensure energy efficiency and affordable electricity, reinforcing domestic demand stability for coal.
